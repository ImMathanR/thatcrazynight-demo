The Code

Who you are
once you're in.

Five rules. Hover any line to read it. Break one and you're out.

  1. 01

    Leave your status at the door.

    Nobody here cares what you do for money, what you drive, or the view from your office. No titles, no degrees, no flexing — and we don't bitch about people who aren't in the room.

  2. 02

    Be a good drunk.

    Good drunks cry, laugh too loud, and make someone else's night. Bad drunks turn mean and wreck it — and they don't come back.

  3. 03

    No perfect photos. Only the drunk ones.

    Phone stays in your pocket. If a photo happens it's blurry, stupid and real — no posing, no stories, no audience. This isn't content.

  4. 04

    Pay your own way.

    $150a night, max
    expect to spend it    And not a cent reaches my pocket — nothing from you, nothing from any bar or business.Everyone covers their own. This was never a business, and it never will be.
  5. 05

    We get everyone home.

    We start together, we leave together. Nobody gets dumped in a gutter or left to sort it out alone. Everyone gets home.

The Crawl

How the night
actually runs.

Three rules. One direction: forward, until 3.

  1. 01

    A pint opens your mind.

    First drink hits the table — and you down it. The Aussies call it skullingskull/skʌl/verb · Australian & NZ informalto drink a glass of beer in one continuous go — no pausing, no breath."he skulled his pint and we were off.", the Swedes shout skål. Skull it on the spot, or you've got four minutes flat. Non-negotiable — that's when the armour comes off and the night starts.

  2. 02

    New bar every hour. A drink at each.

    We don't get comfortable. Every hour we're up and moving, a drink at every stop — your round, your tab. Keep up.

  3. 03

    Stay till 3.

    You're in till 3am. Nobody wants to hear about your workout, your sleep score, or your 9am. You knew the deal — the night gets the night.

The Keeper

Why I
started this.

Over the past year I travelled to X countries and lost count of the nights — all chasing one thing: that crazy, epic night. I've done it all. Became the local to party with the locals, ran with the backpackers, got invited to the big tables — where the bottles are bought just to be seen.

And most of it's dead. People show up to film the DJ and nod their heads. That's dancing? That's fucked. The second I walk into a room like that, I walk straight back out.

Here's what I found: there are more people like me than I thought. People who want a real night, not a highlight reel. So I'm building this to find them — right here in SG.

I just want my epic night back.

Purely selfish, if I'm honest. :)

— the keeper
