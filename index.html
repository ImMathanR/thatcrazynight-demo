The Code

Who you are
once you're in.

Five rules. Hover any line to read it. Break one and you're out.

  1. 01

    Leave your status at the door.

    Nobody here cares what you do for money, what you drive, or the view from your office. No titles, no degrees, no flexing — and we don't bitch about people who aren't in the room.

  2. 02

    Be a good drunk.

    Good drunks cry, laugh too loud, and make someone else's night. Bad drunks turn mean and wreck it — and they don't come back.

  3. 03

    No perfect photos. Only the drunk ones.

    Phone stays in your pocket. If a photo happens it's blurry, stupid and real — no posing, no stories, no audience. This isn't content.

  4. 04

    Pay your own way.

    Everyone covers their own — max $150 a night. Nobody here makes a cent off you. This was never a business.

  5. 05

    We get everyone home.

    We start together, we leave together. Nobody gets dumped in a gutter or left to sort it out alone. Everyone gets home.

The Crawl

How the night
actually runs.

Three rules. One direction: forward, until 3.

  1. 01

    A pint opens your mind.

    First drink hits the table — and you down it. The Aussies call it skulling, the Swedes shout skål. Skull it on the spot, or you've got four minutes flat. Non-negotiable — that's when the armour comes off and the night starts.

    4minutes

  2. 02

    New bar every hour. A drink at each.

    We don't get comfortable. Every hour we're up and moving, a drink at every stop — your round, your tab. Keep up.

  3. 03

    Stay till 3.

    You're in till 3am. Nobody wants to hear about your workout, your sleep score, or your 9am. You knew the deal — the night gets the night.

The Keeper

Why I
started this.

Over the past year I hit ten countries and drank with everyone in them — locals on their own turf, tourists, backpackers, the rich at their reserved tables. Long enough to see exactly what's broken about how Asia parties.

It's all for show. The fine dining for show. The bottle service for show. The expensive table you book so people can watch you sit at it. And worst of all — most people only let go when they're far from home, or stuck with company too boring to care what they think.

I don't buy that. You can chase something real in your life and have a night you'll never forget. I do everything at 100% — work, life, and the drinking too. When I go out, I go hard, and the goal never changes: make it an epic night. So it becomes one.

Partying in SG has flatlined — Clarke Quay is hit or miss these days. So I'm building the night I have everywhere else, right here. Either I throw a few epic nights for the right people, or worst case, I have mine trying.

Either way, the night happens.

— the keeper
